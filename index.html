Here is our Why, What and How

Why We Started Lightening Lives

After over 25 years of experience in the diagnostics field and building strong networks with clinicians, national health ministers, NGOs, academics, and companies, we felt a deep responsibility to give back to all the sections of the society. We realized that the most underserved and vulnerable populations, particularly those from economically weaker sections, are often the ones who need affordable diagnostic services the most. These communities, despite being the most in need, are frequently overlooked when it comes to vital healthcare.

What We Do at Lightening Lives

Our mission is to provide affordable, quick, and robust diagnostic screening tests specifically for hemoglobinopathies and musculopathies. We aim to make these critical tests accessible to underprivileged communities who need them the most, empowering individuals with early detection and timely intervention. Our goal is to empower these populations with early detection, enabling better health outcomes and a higher quality of life.

How We Achieve Our Mission

We leverage our extensive network of clinicians, doctors, NGOs, government sectors, and national health ministries to reach as many individuals as possible. Our approach is focused on improving the scope and logistics of newborn screening, promoting genetic counselling, and screening at-risk populations to minimize or eradicate hemoglobinopathies and musculopathies through carrier counselling and pre-natal diagnosis. Through these efforts, we aim to raise awareness, provide accessible diagnostic services, and ultimately improve the quality of life for individuals affected by these genetic disorders. Additionally, we have developed a breakthrough method that uses just a single drop of blood for molecular screening—minimally invasive and more convenient compared to the large blood volumes collected through more invasive intravenous blood collection in EDTA tubes. This near painless and efficient technique allows us to deliver accurate results within a single day, ensuring faster diagnosis and quicker intervention for the communities that need it most.
